⚖️ Legal Map — Every Wholesaler Must Know This

Equitable interest doctrine: A signed purchase agreement gives you the right to buy — and the right to sell that contract. Selling the property itself without owning it = unlicensed brokerage in every state. Always use "and/or assigns" next to your name and disclose in writing that you hold equitable interest only and intend to assign.

🔴 Red Zone — License Effectively Required

These states have redefined wholesaling as licensed brokerage activity, regardless of whether you market the contract or the property. Avoid completely as an unlicensed beginner.

South Carolina North Carolina Illinois Oklahoma Pennsylvania Kentucky Nebraska

Notable: IL = Class A misdemeanor at 2 deals/year. SC HB 4754 is the hardest — double-closing also restricted. OK SB 1075 (Nov 2025) covers double closings + 2-day seller cancellation right.

🟡 Yellow Zone — Disclosure or Registration Required

No license needed if compliant. Use bold-font written disclosure at or before signing. Each state has its own specific requirements.

Maryland Ohio Tennessee Alabama Indiana Wisconsin Virginia Oregon North Dakota Connecticut Minnesota Iowa Texas Arizona Michigan

Notable: WI = 5-deal annual cap, $5K fine. CT Public Act 25-168 (Jul 1 2026) = $285 fee, 90-day max closing. MI = private marketing only (no public ads).

🟢 Green Zone — Generally Permitted

Wholesaling allowed with proper equitable-interest disclosure. Still use "and/or assigns" and private buyer-list marketing in all green states — enforcement of the public-property-marketing rule is active even where licensing isn't required.

Florida Georgia Missouri Kansas Colorado Washington Nevada California New York New Jersey

FL FREC, GA (Atlanta), CA DRE, and NY Dept. of State have all fined wholesalers for marketing properties they don't own — the public-property-marketing rule is enforced even without a licensing requirement.

Bird-dogging note: Delivering leads for a finder's fee ($500–$2,000 flat per closed deal) is a slightly safer legal posture because you don't sign the contract. But in red-zone states, paying anyone for "procuring" a transaction is still unlicensed brokerage. Use a written marketing/referral agreement with a licensed investor and pay a flat fee — not a percentage of sale price.

The trend: Ten states now have wholesaling-specific laws. Every state will likely have disclosure or licensing rules within two years. Operating cleanly today — written disclosure, "and/or assigns," private buyer-list marketing, investor-friendly title company — costs nothing and future-proofs the business.
